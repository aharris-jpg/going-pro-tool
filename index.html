Key Findings

The main story in this tool is not just talent. It is environment.

School matters more than most athletes think.

Two players in the same sport can face very different odds depending on the school pipeline they enter. Program history changes the baseline dramatically.

Even the best pipelines still produce long odds.

Top schools outperform the national average, but reaching the pros remains rare. That tension between ambition and reality is the core story of the index.

State context helps explain broader opportunity.

Some states consistently outperform others in athlete-to-pro conversion, which helps explain why school odds sit above or below the national average.

Profile inputs nudge the estimate, but do not override the data.

Year, role, and team tier affect the estimate modestly. The school’s historical pipeline remains the foundation of the result.

How We Built This Tool What comes from the underlying school and state datasets, and what this calculator adds. +

This tool starts with school-level progression rates by sport. Each school’s baseline probability reflects historical pro output relative to its estimated athlete pool.

The page then compares that school baseline with the state average and national average for the same sport.

To make the tool more interactive, it also applies a light profile adjustment based on year, playing level, and team tier. These are directional estimate refinements, not guarantees.

In simple terms, the model is: School Probability × Year Multiplier × Playing Level Multiplier × Team Tier Multiplier, with the final result capped so the profile layer cannot overwhelm the school baseline.
